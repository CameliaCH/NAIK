Following up after applying
Hi [Company Name], I'm [Your Name]. I submitted my application for the [Position] role on [Date] and wanted to follow up on the status. I remain very interested in this opportunity. Thank you for your time.
Replace the [ ] brackets with your details before sending.
Asking about your interview outcome
Hi [Name], thank you for interviewing me on [Date] for the [Position] role at [Company]. I enjoyed our conversation and remain very keen on joining the team. Could you kindly let me know if there are any updates? Thank you.
Send this 3–5 working days after your interview if you haven't heard back.
Accepting a job offer
Hi [Name], thank you so much for offering me the [Job Title] position at [Company]. I am happy to accept and look forward to joining the team. Please let me know the next steps — I am ready to start on [Start Date]. Thank you again.
Confirm the start date and any documents needed before replying.
Politely declining an offer
Hi [Name], thank you very much for offering me the [Position] role. After careful consideration, I have decided to pursue a different opportunity at this time. I truly appreciate the time you invested in me and hope we may have the chance to connect again in the future.
Always decline politely — Malaysia's job market is small and reputations matter.
Asking a previous employer for a reference
Hi [Name], I hope you are doing well. I am currently applying for a new position and would be very grateful if you could serve as a reference for me. If you are comfortable, a potential employer may contact you to ask about my work during my time at [Company]. Please let me know if this is okay. Thank you very much.
Always ask permission before listing someone as a reference.

These rights are yours under Malaysian law. You do not need to ask for them — your employer is legally required to provide them.

🤱
Maternity Leave
98 days paid
For each of your first 5 children. You must be paid at your full normal rate. Your employer cannot dismiss you, force a resignation, or demote you while you are pregnant or on leave.
Employment Act 1955, s.37
🚫
No Pregnancy Dismissal
Illegal to fire you
It is illegal to terminate your employment, force you to resign, or change your job scope/pay because you are pregnant, had a miscarriage, or gave birth. Lodge a complaint with JTKSM.
Employment Act 1955, s.37B
🍼
Breastfeeding Breaks
Up to 2 years
Your employer must allow nursing breaks at work for up to 2 years after childbirth. You cannot be penalised, docked pay, or disciplined for requesting these breaks.
Employment Act 1955, s.37A
🛑
Anti-Sexual Harassment
Zero tolerance
Unwanted verbal, non-verbal, or physical conduct of a sexual nature in the workplace is illegal. You can lodge a confidential complaint with the Anti-Sexual Harassment Tribunal — no fee required.
Anti-Sexual Harassment Act 2022 (ASHA)
💊
SOCSO Maternity Benefit
Medical coverage + EIS
SOCSO covers medical expenses related to childbirth. If you lose your job while pregnant or after returning, the Employment Insurance System (EIS) provides a job loss benefit. Check via the PERKESO Assist app.
SOCSO / EIS — PERKESO
👶
TASKA Childcare Subsidy
Up to RM 300 / month
Working mothers can apply for monthly childcare subsidies at JKM-registered TASKA (Taman Asuhan Kanak-Kanak) centres. Subsidies are means-tested and available nationwide. Apply at your nearest JKM office.
Jabatan Kebajikan Masyarakat (JKM)
🚨 If any of these rights are violated — contact the Department of Labour (JTKSM) free hotline: 1800-88-5751 or lodge a complaint at e-aduan.jtksm.gov.my. For sexual harassment, contact the Anti-Sexual Harassment Tribunal via SUHAKAM. All complaints are confidential.
Before you take on freelance or gig work, run through this checklist. These steps protect your time, money, and safety — especially as a woman working independently.
Vetting a new client or employer
☐
Search the company on SSM BizSearch — confirm they are a registered business
☐
Request a written contract or agreement before starting any work
☐
Payment terms confirmed in writing — amount, method, and due date
☐
Verified they have a real website, physical address, or office number
☐
Checked Google or Glassdoor reviews for the company
☐
Know your direct contact's full name and official email
🚩 Walk away immediately if they...
  • Ask you to pay a registration fee, training deposit, or any upfront cost
  • Refuse to provide a written contract or agreement
  • Communicate via WhatsApp only — no company email or official channel
  • Keep changing the job description after you agree
  • Ask you to work unpaid "as a trial" for more than 1–2 days
  • Pressure you to start immediately before you have signed anything
  • Offer unusually high pay with vague or secretive duties
  • Ask you to meet alone in a private location for "training" or "orientation"
Payment protection
☐
For freelance projects: requested 30–50% payment upfront before starting
☐
Issued a written invoice — kept a copy as evidence
☐
Payment due date agreed and written down
☐
For gig platforms (Grab, Lalamove, etc.): read the payment release policy before accepting
☐
Never shared banking login details or TAC codes with a client
☐
Using bank transfer or platform payment — not cash where possible
💰 If you are not paid on time...
1
Send a formal written demand via WhatsApp or email. State the amount owed, the original due date, and give 7 days to pay.
2
If no response, file a claim at the Tribunal Tuntutan Pengguna Malaysia (consumer claims tribunal) for amounts up to RM 50,000 — fee is only RM 5.
3
For employment disputes, contact JTKSM: 1800-88-5751 (free hotline). Keep all WhatsApp messages and invoices as evidence.
