Following up after applying
Hi [Company Name], I'm [Your Name]. I submitted my application for the [Position] role on [Date] and wanted to follow up on the status. I remain very interested in this opportunity. Thank you for your time.
Replace the [ ] brackets with your details before sending.
Asking about your interview outcome
Hi [Name], thank you for interviewing me on [Date] for the [Position] role at [Company]. I enjoyed our conversation and remain very keen on joining the team. Could you kindly let me know if there are any updates? Thank you.
Send this 3–5 working days after your interview if you haven't heard back.
Accepting a job offer
Hi [Name], thank you so much for offering me the [Job Title] position at [Company]. I am happy to accept and look forward to joining the team. Please let me know the next steps — I am ready to start on [Start Date]. Thank you again.
Confirm the start date and any documents needed before replying.
Politely declining an offer
Hi [Name], thank you very much for offering me the [Position] role. After careful consideration, I have decided to pursue a different opportunity at this time. I truly appreciate the time you invested in me and hope we may have the chance to connect again in the future.
Always decline politely — Malaysia's job market is small and reputations matter.
Asking a previous employer for a reference
Hi [Name], I hope you are doing well. I am currently applying for a new position and would be very grateful if you could serve as a reference for me. If you are comfortable, a potential employer may contact you to ask about my work during my time at [Company]. Please let me know if this is okay. Thank you very much.
Always ask permission before listing someone as a reference.

💰
Minimum Wage
RM 1,700 / month
As of February 2023 for all employers with 5 or more workers. RM 1,500/month for employers with fewer than 5 workers.
🕐
Working Hours
Max 48 hrs / week
No more than 8 hours a day, 6 days a week. Overtime must be paid — 1.5× on weekdays, 2× on rest days, 3× on public holidays.
🌴
Annual Leave
8 – 16 days
Less than 2 years: 8 days. 2–5 years: 12 days. More than 5 years: 16 days. Your employer cannot take this away.
🤒
Sick Leave
14 – 22 days
Less than 2 years: 14 days. 2–5 years: 18 days. More than 5 years: 22 days. Hospitalisation adds up to 60 days on top.
🛡️
EPF & SOCSO
Mandatory contributions
Your employer must contribute to EPF (KWSP) and SOCSO (PERKESO) on your behalf. If they don't, this is illegal. Check your EPF via i-Akaun app.
🤱
Maternity Leave
98 days (paid)
For the first 5 children. You cannot be dismissed while on maternity leave. Breastfeeding breaks must be allowed for up to 2 years.
🚨 If your employer breaks these rules — contact the Department of Labour (JTKSM) free hotline: 1800-88-5751 or lodge a complaint at e-aduan.jtksm.gov.my. Your complaint is confidential.
